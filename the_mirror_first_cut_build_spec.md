# The Mirror — First-Cut Build Spec
*A hand-it-to-an-AI-builder blueprint. Working name "The Mirror." Test cohort: your daughter + one friend's child.*

---

## How to use this document

You're building with AI, not by hand. So this spec gives you the **logic and content** an AI builder needs, not code. Paste sections into your builder as you go. The golden rule that governs every screen:

> **Build every layer in the engine room. Show the kid only the light daily drop.** Capability goes big; the kid's surface stays a 30-second thing. If a screen makes the kid feel studied, assessed, or graded — it's wrong, no matter how clever the engine behind it.

**Banned words in all kid-facing copy:** assessment, test, score, evaluation, result, grade, personality type, strength/weakness. Use: notice, spot, gather, map, explore, what pulls you.

---

## 1. What the first cut is

A two-sided web app (mobile-first) with a shared brain:

- **Kid side** — a daily 30-second "drop" + a gentle weekly mirror she edits.
- **Parent side** — a 2–3×/week light observation log (their *own* view) + the periodic compare and trade-offs.
- **The brain** — an AI engine that reads everything, extracts interest "ingredients," tracks durability signals over time, and—only when asked—generates illuminated trade-offs. It never names a stream or a career.

Three things make it different from every existing tool: it reads **free expression** (not MCQs), it separates **durable from passing** over time, and it stops at **illuminated trade-offs** so it never becomes the decision-maker.

---

## 2. What this test will and won't prove

| Provable now (1 summer, 2 kids) | NOT provable now |
|---|---|
| Will a kid log unprompted? (the habit) | Whether the durability read is *accurate* (needs months) |
| Does the AI extract ingredients the kid recognizes? | Statistical patterns (n=2 → depth, not patterns) |
| Do the weekly mirrors earn "huh, I didn't realize that"? | Retention past the novelty window |
| Does the parent compare reveal real blind spots? | Whether it changes an actual class-11 decision |

Watch your two kids closely and qualitatively. You're hunting for *moments*, not metrics.

## 3. The engine room (all layers — runs behind the scenes)

### Layer A — Kid Capture (the only thing the kid mostly sees)
A daily push: one rotating prompt, answered by **text or voice** (voice auto-transcribed). One prompt a day. Never more. Skippable with no guilt.

**Daily prompt bank (rotate, don't repeat within a week):**
1. What made you lose track of time today?
2. What did you choose to do when nothing was required of you?
3. What made you curious — something you wanted to understand better?
4. What drained you or made you want to leave the room?
5. What did someone come to you for, or thank you for?
6. What annoyed you that other people didn't seem to mind? *(signals taste/standards)*
7. What did you make, fix, build, or figure out?
8. What did you watch/read/scroll that you actually went deeper on afterward?

**Weekly "why" prompt (deeper, once a week):**
> "Pick one thing from this week you kept coming back to. Why that one? What was it about it?"
*(This single question is your richest durability + motivation signal — the "why" underneath the "what.")*

### Layer B — Parent Capture (separate view, never overwrites the kid)
A 2–3×/week nudge to the parent:
1. What did you see [kid] gravitate toward this week, unprompted?
2. What did she light up talking about?
3. What did she avoid or drag her feet on?
4. Did anything surprise you about her this week?

Logged into the **parent's own mirror.** The kid does not see it until the compare moment, and vice versa.

### Layer C — Decipher Engine (AI)
For every kid entry, the AI does three jobs. *(System-prompt logic to give your builder:)*

> Read this entry from a 13–16 year old. Do NOT judge or score. Extract:
> 1. **Interest ingredients** — the underlying pulls, not surface activities. Use a controlled tag set: *building/making, understanding-why/systems, helping-others-understand, caring-for-people, organizing/structuring, performing/expressing, competing/winning, aesthetics/craft, storytelling/narrative, exploring/novelty, persuading/leading, analyzing/numbers, nature/living-things, hands-on/physical.* (Extend only when truly needed.)
> 2. **The motivation underneath** — why this might matter to them (mastery? recognition? helping? curiosity? belonging?).
> 3. **Durability signals present in THIS entry** (see Layer D).
> Output as structured tags + one plain-language sentence the kid would recognize. Never output a label, type, or recommendation.

### Layer D — Durability Engine (THE DIFFERENTIATOR)
For each recurring interest, track six signals **over time**. This is a *profile that accumulates*, never a one-shot score.

| Signal | Reads as durable when… | Reads as passing when… |
|---|---|---|
| Self-initiation | returns to it unprompted, on own time | only appears when triggered externally |
| Recurrence across contexts | shows up in different activities | welded to one single trigger |
| Survives friction | sticks past boredom/plateau/difficulty | dies at first hard part |
| Depth-seeking | wants to understand why / get better | stays surface/aesthetic only |
| Origin | older or multiple roots | traces to one recent spark (show, friend, trend) |
| Energy over repetition | keeps energizing on repeat | curdles to drain once novelty fades |

**How it's shown (honest, non-judgmental labels):**
- 🌱 *Still exploring* — too new to tell. **(Celebrated — exploring is healthy and normal.)**
- 🌿 *Showing staying power* — durable signals accumulating across weeks.
- 🍃 *Looks trigger-bound* — tied to a recent spark; hasn't outlived it yet.

**Mandatory framing rule:** durable-vs-passing is NEVER "real vs fake" or "good vs bad." It is *"proven enough to weight in the class-11 decision"* vs *"still exploring — don't bet the stream on it yet."* A passing phase is exploration, not a mistake.

### Layer E — Reflect (weekly mirror, kid edits)
Once a week, the kid sees a gentle "what I'm noticing about you" — 2–3 observations framed as **questions, not verdicts**:
> "Looks like figuring out *why* things work pulled you in three times this week — does that feel true?"

Kid taps: *yes / sort of / not really* and can edit. Her edits update her portrait. **Her reconciliation is the product's engine** — she always owns the final picture.

### Layer F — Compare (the pair, periodically)
Every ~2–3 weeks, kid portrait and parent observations shown **side by side**:
- **Matches** → affirm ("you both see this").
- **Gaps** → the gold. Parent-sees-but-kid-doesn't = a hidden strength delivered as a gift. Kid-feels-but-parent-missed = the kid finally feeling seen.
- Ends with **one conversation prompt** for them to talk through together (not a verdict to receive).

### Layer G — Illuminated Trade-offs (the payoff — on demand / at decision time)
Only surfaces when the family chooses, or near the class-10→11 decision. For the kid's **durable** interest clusters, the AI generates a trade-off view. **Template per direction:**
> **If you leaned toward [Science / Commerce / Humanities / a specific field]:**
> - *What it tends to involve day-to-day:* …
> - *What it would ask you to push through:* … (the hard, boring, or draining parts — honestly)
> - *What it tends to open up later:* …
> - *How it connects to what we've seen pull you:* … (ties to her actual durable interests)
> - *What it might cost / close off:* …

**Hard rule:** present **at least two directions side by side, never one.** End with: *"There's no right answer here — this is to help you both see clearly, not to choose for you."* The product never says "take X."

### Layer H — Role Models (relatable, not celebrities)
For durable interest clusters, surface 2–3 **relatable** real people who took a similar path — explicitly including **someone who took the path and then changed course**, so it reads "paths are real and flexible," not "become this person."
- *First-cut MVP:* hand-curate a small set yourself (10–15 short profiles) rather than building a database. Honest, fast, good enough to test the reaction.
- *Decision you still owe:* does "known to me" mean the **family network** (cousin, neighbour) or **relatable-to-the-kid** (a reachable-feeling creator)? Pick one for the test.

---

## 4. The kid's actual experience (keep it this small)
Day to day, the kid sees **only**: a daily prompt → she answers in 30 sec → done. Once a week: a gentle mirror she can tap through and edit. That's it. Everything in §3 C/D/F/G/H happens invisibly and surfaces *occasionally and gently*. If the kid ever feels the weight of the engine, you've broken the product.

---

## 5. Tone & safety (non-negotiable)
- No scores or numbers shown to the kid, ever.
- No personality typing. No psychology diagnosis. Observe interests, influences, traction — nothing deeper.
- Everything about the kid is editable by the kid.
- Parent contributes a *parallel view*; never edits or overrides the kid's.
- Keep it warm, curious, never clinical. It's a mirror, not a microscope.

---

## 6. Tech recommendation (for an AI builder)
You don't need much. A lightweight path:
- **Front end:** a simple mobile-friendly web app (an AI app-builder or a basic React app). Two logins: kid, parent.
- **Storage:** any simple database (entries, tags, durability profiles, portraits).
- **The brain:** calls to an LLM with the system-prompt logic in §3C/D/G. This is where the real product lives — the AI *is* the decipher + durability + trade-offs engine.
- **Notifications:** a daily push/reminder for the kid, 2–3×/week for the parent.
Start with the two of you hand-running the AI parts if needed; automate once the flow feels right.

---

## 7. Test instrumentation (so "go big" still teaches you)
Quietly log, for each of the 2 kids:
- Days logged unprompted (habit signal).
- % of AI-extracted ingredients the kid taps "yes, that's me" (decipher accuracy — target >70%).
- Count of "huh, I didn't realize that" moments (you'll note these by hand).
- One blind-spot the parent compare revealed.
- Anything that made the kid roll her eyes or quit — *the most valuable data you'll get.*

---

## 8. The one risk to stay honest about
Your differentiator (durable-vs-passing) is a **time-series** problem — it's defensible *because* it's slow, but slow fights retention. So your hardest unsolved design problem isn't in this spec: **what keeps a family opening this in weeks 1–4 while the durability signal is still thin?** (Working answer: the weekly mirror + the compare moments are the interim delight; durability is the compounding payoff. Validate that the *interim* delight is real with your two kids — if weeks 1–4 don't delight, the magic never gets a chance to accrue.)
