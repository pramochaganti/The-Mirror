What is The Mirror,
and what do you do?

A two-minute read before you start. No tests, no grades — just noticing what pulls you in.

What it actually is
"It's not about figuring out what you want to do with your life. It's just about noticing what you're already drawn to — and letting that picture build up over time."

The Mirror asks you one question a day. Your answer goes into a quiet AI that spots patterns in what pulls your attention — not to label you, but to show you things you might not have noticed yourself. Over weeks, a picture builds. You own it. You edit it. You decide what's true.

How it works, day to day
☀️
Open the app once a day. One question appears — what pulled you today, what you chose to do when no one was watching, what made you curious.
✍️
Answer in a line or two — or speak it out loud. Short, honest, unpolished. That's exactly what the app needs.
✅
Done. Takes 30 seconds. Come back tomorrow.
🪞
Once a week, the app shows you 2–3 gentle observations about what it's noticing. You tap yes, sort of, or not really — and edit anything that feels off. Your edit is what stays.
🌿
Over time, your "Pulls" tab fills up — interests the app has seen you return to, with a sense of whether they're just sprouting or genuinely taking root.
Do's and don'ts
✓ Do
✓Answer honestly — even if the answer feels small or embarrassing. "I spent 2 hours watching paint dry videos" is useful. "I studied hard" is not.
✓Keep it short. Two sentences is plenty. The app doesn't need essays.
✓Edit or delete any entry that doesn't feel right. Everything here is yours to change.
✓Disagree with the weekly mirror when it gets it wrong. Your "not really" is the most useful data point it can get.
✓Skip days. There's no punishment. The streak resets; the picture doesn't disappear.
✗ Don't
✗Write what sounds impressive or what you think a parent wants to see. It only works on real answers.
✗Stress about being consistent. Open it when you feel like it. Even 3 days a week is enough.
✗Treat the weekly mirror as a verdict. It's a question, not a label. If it feels wrong, say so.
✗Share your phone with a parent during your session. Your entries are yours — they can't see them unless you choose to show them.
✗Expect instant answers. This is a slow-build thing. The interesting stuff shows up in weeks 3–6.
Your privacy
🔒 Your entries live only on your phone. An AI reads each one to find patterns — it isn't stored on any server. Your parent can't see what you write unless you decide to show them. You can delete everything with one tap, any time.
The one thing to remember
This isn't about figuring out the "right" stream. It's about building a clearer picture of what genuinely pulls you — so that whatever decision comes, it feels like yours. 💛
